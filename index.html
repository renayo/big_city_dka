Birth Timing & Yoga Prevalence

Does the empirical difference in birth-hour distributions between large urban hospitals (high obstetric intervention) and small-town hospitals (biological circadian rhythm) produce detectable differences in Jyotish yoga rates?

Key finding
Panchamahapurusha yogas show no significant difference between groups — planetary cycle timing dominates over birth-hour distribution. Three Dharma Karmadhipati conditions are highly significant after Bonferroni correction (omnibus χ² = 47.34, p < 0.001). Large-city daytime birth clustering raises rates of sign-exchange, own-house placement, and 9th/10th conjunction.

Why is Lagna not significantly different? The Ascendant depends on sidereal time at the birth location — which part of the ecliptic is rising at that moment. Large-city births cluster in daytime hours, over-representing signs that rise during the day at US mid-latitudes. But all 12 signs rise once per 24 hours, so even a heavily daytime-skewed distribution still produces a recognisable spread across all signs. At N = 10,000, chi-square cannot distinguish the slight frequency differences.

Why no effect? Panchamahapurusha yogas require a planet in its own or exalted sign and in a kendra from the Lagna. The yoga is determined almost entirely by the planet's current sidereal sign — a slow-moving position set by calendar date alone (Jupiter: 12-year cycle; Saturn: 29.5-year cycle). Changing the birth-hour distribution shifts the Lagna distribution, but since all 12 Lagna signs still appear in both groups and planetary signs are identical on any given date, the yoga rate is essentially unchanged.

Mechanism. Large-city births cluster heavily in daytime hours (73.6% between 6am and 6pm). At US mid-latitudes (~30–42°N), the signs Cancer through Sagittarius rise primarily during daylight — over-representing those six signs as the Lagna in the large-city group. This shifts which signs become the 9th and 10th houses and therefore where their lords tend to fall.

The Exchange, Own Houses, and Conjunction conditions are sensitive to whether a lord falls in a specific house-sign. The broader conditions — Conjunction Anywhere and Mutual Aspect — are not sign-specific in the same way and show no effect.

Research implication. Celebrity and notable-person birth data skews strongly toward large urban hospitals. Any study comparing Dharma Karmadhipati yoga rates between a celebrity sample and a general-population baseline must account for the birth-time distribution difference — a previously uncharacterised confound in Jyotish statistical research.
