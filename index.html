Birth Timing & Yoga Prevalence

Does the empirical difference in birth-hour distributions between large urban hospitals (high obstetric intervention) and small-town hospitals (biological circadian rhythm) produce detectable differences in Jyotish yoga rates?

Key finding
Panchamahapurusha yogas: no significant difference — planetary-cycle dominance overwhelms the birth-time effect. Dharma Karmadhipati yogas: three conditions are highly significant after Bonferroni correction (omnibus χ²=47.34, p<0.001). Large-city daytime birth clustering systematically raises rates of sign-exchange, own-house, and 9th/10th conjunction conditions.

Sidereal Lagna sign distribution — χ²=9.74   p=0.554   not significant

Despite a 16-hour difference in mean birth time between groups, Lagna profiles are statistically indistinguishable at N=10,000. The 12-sign averaging absorbs the distributional effect.

Why is Lagna not significantly different? The Ascendant depends on sidereal time at the birth location — which part of the sky is rising at that moment. Large-city births cluster in daytime hours, over-representing signs that rise during the day at US mid-latitudes. But because all 12 signs rise once every 24 hours, even a heavily daytime-skewed distribution still produces a recognizable spread across all signs. At N=10,000, chi-square cannot distinguish the slight frequency differences.

Why no effect? Panchamahapurusha yogas require a planet in its own or exalted sign AND in a kendra from the Lagna. Yoga presence is determined almost entirely by the planet's current sidereal sign — a slow position set by calendar date alone (Jupiter: 12-year cycle; Saturn: 29.5-year cycle). Changing the birth-hour distribution shifts the Lagna distribution, but since all 12 Lagna signs still appear in both groups and planetary signs are identical on any given date, the yoga rate is effectively unchanged.

Mechanism. Large-city births cluster in daytime hours (73.6% between 6am and 6pm). At US mid-latitudes (~30–42°N), the signs Cancer through Sagittarius rise primarily during daylight hours. This over-represents those six signs as the Lagna in the large-city group, shifting which signs become the 9th and 10th houses and therefore where their lords tend to fall.

The Exchange, Own Houses, and Conjunction conditions are sensitive to whether a lord falls in a specific sign (the 9th or 10th). When those houses are systematically biased toward certain signs, the likelihood of exact positional alignment changes. Broader conditions — Conjunction Anywhere and Mutual Aspect — are not sign-specific in the same way and show no effect.

Implication for Jyotish research. Celebrity and notable-person birth data skews strongly toward large urban hospitals. Any study comparing Dharma Karmadhipati yoga rates between a celebrity sample and a general-population baseline must account for the birth-time distribution difference. This is a previously uncharacterized confound.
